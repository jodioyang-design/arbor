IC PLAYBOOK19

The 3-Meeting Conversion Framework

Meeting 1: DISCOVERY
60 min • Listen 70%, talk 30%
Goal: Understand their world

Key questions:
• “What’s your current Asia/VC allocation?”
• “How has your PE program evolved?”
• “What does your IC process look like?”
• “Who else is involved in the decision?”

DO NOT: Present the full deck.
Outcome: Customize all subsequent materials to their priorities.
Meeting 2: TAILORED PITCH
90 min • Structured presentation
Structure:
• 10 min: “Based on our conversation, here’s what I heard...”
• 30 min: Fund overview tailored to their mandate
• 20 min: Portfolio deep dive on THEIR-relevant companies
• 15 min: Track record with THEIR peer benchmarks
• 15 min: Q&A and next steps

Key: Reference THEIR allocation targets, THEIR peers, THEIR stated priorities.
Meeting 3: IC PREP
45 min • Arm the champion
Provide:
• Pre-filled IC memo template
• Peer comparison (who committed, who’s looking)
• Risk-mitigation talking points
• DDQ, ODD, reference contacts

Ask: “What objections will come up at IC? Let me prepare responses.”

Goal: Make it impossible for the champion to fail internally.

IC PLAYBOOK21

The Hidden Variable:
Career Risk

“No allocator was ever fired for investing in Sequoia. Every LP decision carries personal career risk. Your job is to de-risk their decision.”

1. SOCIAL PROOF
“12 insurance companies are already Arbor LPs across Funds I-III” — MetLife, Tokio Marine, AXA, Fidelity, Thiel Capital. Name recognition reduces perceived risk.
2. PEER BENCHMARKING
“U Michigan committed $75M to Sequoia China. TCDRS committed $70M to Source Code/IDG. Your peers are already in Asia VC.” No one wants to be the last institution to a proven category.
3. ASYMMETRIC FRAMING
“Fund I returned 2.3x DPI — that’s cash back to LPs while most VC funds are at 0x DPI. The risk isn’t investing; it’s missing the next Tabby (23.6x).” Frame the risk of inaction.
4. INTERNAL CHAMPION SUPPORT
“I will prepare your IC memo, draft your board talking points, and be available for your IC call.” Make it impossible for the allocator to fail internally.
